What we help you with

Breathing problems

This is a general term for any difficulty inhaling or exhaling air. It can range from mild shortness of breath to severe respiratory distress. There are many causes, including infections, allergies, lung diseases, and heart problems.

Asthma

A chronic (long-term) lung disease that inflames and narrows the airways. This makes breathing difficult, especially during exercise or exposure to triggers like allergens or irritants. Symptoms include wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath. Asthma is treatable, but not curable.

Post cardiac surgery

This refers to the recovery period after heart surgery. During this time, you may experience some breathing difficulties due to pain, fluid buildup around the lungs (pleural effusion), or changes in lung function caused by the surgery itself. Medications, breathing exercises, and physical therapy can help manage these issues.

COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease)

A group of lung diseases that obstruct airflow and make breathing difficult. The most common types are emphysema and chronic bronchitis. COPD is often caused by smoking and there is no cure, but treatments can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life. These treatments include medications, breathing exercises, and pulmonary rehabilitation.

What we help you with

Shoulder Pain

Causes: Rotator cuff injuries, bursitis, arthritis, impingement syndrome, muscle strains, and referred pain from neck problems. Symptoms: Pain, tenderness, weakness, limited range of motion, and difficulty sleeping on the affected side.

Knee Pain

Causes: Arthritis, meniscus tears, ligament sprains, overuse injuries, bursitis, and referred pain from hip or back problems. Symptoms: Pain, swelling, stiffness, instability, clicking or popping sounds, and difficulty bending or straightening the knee.

Elbow Pain

Causes: Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis), golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis), bursitis, arthritis, and overuse injuries. Symptoms: Pain, tenderness, weakness, difficulty gripping or lifting objects, and pain radiating down the forearm.

Back Pain

Causes: Muscle strains, disc herniation, arthritis, poor posture, and referred pain from other areas like the hips or pelvis. Symptoms: Pain, stiffness, muscle spasms, difficulty moving, and pain radiating down the legs (sciatica).

Wrist Pain

Causes: Carpal tunnel syndrome, arthritis, tendonitis, sprains, and fractures. Symptoms: Pain, numbness, tingling, weakness, and difficulty gripping or making a fist.

Ankle Pain

Causes: Sprains, strains, fractures, arthritis, tendonitis, and Achilles tendon problems. Symptoms: Pain, swelling, bruising, difficulty walking or bearing weight, and instability.

What we help you with

Global Developmental Delay (GDD)

Global developmental delay (GDD) is a term used when a child shows delays in two or more areas of development. GDD can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetic conditions, brain injuries, infections, and developmental disorders. Early diagnosis and intervention are important for improving a child's development and quality of life.

Cerebral Palsy (CP)

Cerebral palsy (CP) is a group of disorders that affect movement and posture. It is caused by damage to the developing brain, most often before birth. There is no cure for CP, but early intervention and therapy can help children with CP reach their full potential.

Muscular Dystrophy (MD)

Muscular dystrophy (MD) is a group of genetic disorders that cause progressive weakness and breakdown of skeletal muscles over time. There are many different types of MD, each with its own pattern of muscle weakness and progression. There is no cure for MD, but treatments can help manage symptoms and slow the progression of the disease

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a complex developmental condition that affects how people interact with others, communicate, learn, and behave. People with ASD may have difficulty with social interaction, communication, and repetitive behaviors. Symptoms of ASD can vary widely

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. Symptoms of ADHD can vary widely. ADHD can be diagnosed and treated by a mental health professional. Treatment may include medication, therapy, and behavioral interventions.

Spine

The spine, also known as the vertebral column, is a series of connected bones (vertebrae) running from the base of the skull to the tailbone, providing essential support for the body while protecting the spinal cord within its bony canal. It is divided into distinct regions including the cervical spine (neck), thoracic spine (mid-back), and lumbar spine (lower back), each contributing to a range of motion depending on the structure of its vertebrae and joints.

What we help you with

Cervical Spondylosis

What it is: A general term for age-related changes in the cervical spine (neck). The discs between the vertebrae degenerate, leading to bone spurs and narrowing of the spinal canal. Symptoms: Neck pain and stiffness, headaches, numbness or tingling in the arms or hands, muscle weakness, and difficulty walking.

Lumbar Spondylosis

What it is: Similar to cervical spondylosis, but affects the lumbar spine (lower back). Symptoms: Lower back pain, stiffness, numbness or tingling in the legs or feet, and muscle weakness.

Spondylitis

What it is: An umbrella term for inflammatory conditions affecting the spine. Types: Ankylosing spondylitis is a type of spondylitis that causes inflammation of the spine and sacroiliac joints. Symptoms: Back pain, stiffness, fatigue, and difficulty moving.

Spondylisthesis

What it is: Occurs when one vertebra slips forward onto the vertebra below it. Symptoms: Back pain, numbness or tingling in the legs or feet, and muscle weakness.

Ankylosing Spondylitis

What it is: A type of inflammatory arthritis that primarily affects the spine and sacroiliac joints. Symptoms: Back pain and stiffness, especially in the morning, fatigue, and difficulty moving.

Sciatica

What it is: Pain that radiates down the leg due to compression of the sciatic nerve. Causes: Often caused by a herniated disc or spinal stenosis. Symptoms: Pain, numbness, tingling, and weakness in the leg or foot.

Disc Bulge

What it is: Occurs when the outer layer of a spinal disc weakens and bulges outward, potentially compressing nerves. Symptoms: Back pain, numbness or tingling, and muscle weakness.

Scoliosis

What it is: A sideways curvature of the spine. Symptoms: Uneven shoulders or hips, back pain, and difficulty breathing in severe cases.

Kyphosis/Lordosis

What it is: Kyphosis is an excessive forward curvature of the spine (hunchback), while lordosis is an excessive inward curvature (swayback). Symptoms: Back pain, difficulty breathing, and postural problems.

What we help you with

PCOD/PCOS:

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age. It's characterized by irregular periods, excessive hair growth, acne, and weight gain. PCOS can lead to infertility, type 2 diabetes, and heart disease.

Hysterectomy:

This is a surgical procedure to remove the uterus. It's often performed to treat conditions like heavy menstrual bleeding, uterine fibroids, endometriosis, and uterine cancer.

Previous Pelvic Surgery:

This refers to any prior surgical procedure performed on the pelvic region, which includes the uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, and bladder. Such surgeries can lead to complications like scarring, adhesion formation, and chronic pelvic pain.

Uterine Prolapse:

This occurs when the uterus slips down from its normal position in the pelvis. It can cause symptoms like pelvic pressure, urinary or bowel problems, and discomfort during sexual intercourse.

Post-Pregnancy Weight Gain:

Weight gain after pregnancy is common. However, excessive weight gain can lead to various health issues, including obesity, diabetes, and heart disease.

Post-Pregnancy Low Back Pain:

Hormonal changes, weakened core muscles, and the physical stress of pregnancy and childbirth can contribute to low back pain.

Diastasis Recti:

This condition occurs when the two muscles that run down the front of your abdomen separate. It's often caused by pregnancy and can lead to a bulging belly, lower back pain, and digestive issues.

Constipation:

This is a common digestive problem characterized by infrequent or difficult bowel movements. It can be exacerbated by hormonal changes, dietary factors, and lack of physical activity.

Urinary/Bowel Incontinence:

This refers to the involuntary leakage of urine or stool. It can be caused by various factors, including weakened pelvic floor muscles, nerve damage, and certain medical conditions.

Cancer

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells. These cells can invade 1 and destroy healthy tissues, potentially spreading to other parts of the body. While various factors can contribute to cancer development, including genetics and lifestyle choices, early detection and treatment are crucial for improving outcomes.

What we help you with

Post Cancer Surgery

This refers to the period following a surgical procedure to remove cancerous tissue. Recovery can involve pain management, wound care, and physical therapy. Potential long-term effects may include scarring, nerve damage, and lymphedema.

Post Chemo Therapy

The time after chemotherapy treatment, often marked by fatigue, hair loss, and weakened immune system. Side effects can vary widely and may persist for months or even years. Regular check-ups are essential to monitor for delayed effects.

Lymphedema

A chronic condition that occurs when lymph fluid builds up in tissues, often in the arms or legs. It can cause swelling, pain, and discomfort. Lymphedema frequently develops after cancer treatment, especially surgery or radiation therapy that damages lymph nodes.

Post Radio Therapy

The period following radiation therapy, a cancer treatment that uses high-energy rays to kill cancer cells. Common side effects include skin irritation, fatigue, and hair loss. Long-term effects may include scarring, damage to internal organs, and secondary cancers.

Post Breast Cancer

The time after a breast cancer diagnosis and treatment. It may involve physical and emotional challenges, including body image issues, fertility concerns, and increased risk of other health problems. Regular check-ups and screenings are crucial for early detection of recurrence.

Surgery/Chemo

A common cancer treatment approach combining surgery to remove cancerous tissue followed by chemotherapy to eliminate any remaining cancer cells. This combined therapy can improve overall survival rates but may increase the risk of side effects.

What we help you with

Total Knee Replacement

Total knee replacement surgery is a surgical procedure to replace a damaged knee joint with an artificial one. This is often done to relieve pain and improve function in individuals with severe arthritis or other knee joint conditions. The procedure involves removing damaged cartilage and bone and replacing them with artificial components.

Total Hip Replacement

Total hip replacement surgery is a surgical procedure to replace a damaged hip joint with an artificial one. This is commonly done to relieve pain and improve mobility in individuals with severe hip arthritis or other hip joint conditions. The procedure involves removing damaged cartilage and bone in the hip joint and replacing them with artificial components.

Ligament Injury/Reconstruction

Ligament injuries can occur in various joints, such as the knee, ankle, or elbow. These injuries can range from mild sprains to complete tears. Ligament reconstruction surgery is a surgical procedure to repair a torn ligament using tissue grafts. This procedure aims to restore joint stability and function.

Post-Fracture Implants

Post-fracture implants are devices used to stabilize broken bones during the healing process. These implants can include plates, screws, rods, or pins. They are surgically inserted into the bone to hold the broken pieces in place, promoting proper healing and preventing further damage

Meniscus Injury

The meniscus is a cartilage disc in the knee joint that acts as a shock absorber. Meniscus injuries can occur due to trauma or degenerative changes. Treatment for meniscus injuries may include conservative measures like physical therapy or surgical intervention, such as meniscus repair or removal.

Post-Fracture Rehabilitation

Post-fracture rehabilitation is a crucial part of the recovery process after a bone fracture. It involves a series of exercises and therapies designed to restore strength, flexibility, and range of motion to the injured limb. Physical therapy plays a key role in guiding patients through the rehabilitation process and helping them regain their pre-injury function.

What we help you with

Strabismus (Crossed Eyes)

Strabismus occurs when the eyes don't align properly, causing one eye to turn inward, outward, upward, or downward. It can lead to double vision, poor depth perception, and eye strain. Treatment often involves eyeglasses, eye exercises, patching, or surgery.

Diplopia (Double Vision)

Diplopia is a condition where a person sees two images of a single object. It can result from various factors like weak eye muscles, refractive errors, cataracts, or neurological disorders. Treatment depends on the underlying cause and may include corrective lenses, prism glasses, or surgery.

Amblyopia (Lazy Eye)

Amblyopia is a condition where one eye becomes weaker than the other due to poor vision development. It often affects children and can lead to permanent vision loss in the weaker eye if not treated early. Treatment typically involves patching the stronger eye to force the weaker eye to work harder.

Digital Eye Strain

Digital eye strain is a common condition caused by prolonged use of digital devices. Symptoms include eye fatigue, headaches, blurred vision, dry eyes, and neck and shoulder pain. To reduce strain, it's important to take regular breaks, adjust screen brightness and contrast, and practice good posture.

Eye Strengthening Before Surgery

Eye strengthening exercises can be beneficial before certain eye surgeries, such as cataract surgery. These exercises can improve eye muscle strength and coordination, aiding in a smoother recovery and better post-operative vision.

Squint Eye

A squint eye, or strabismus, is a condition where the eyes don't align properly. It can be caused by various factors and may require treatment to prevent vision problems.

Convergence Insufficiency

Convergence insufficiency is a condition where the eyes have difficulty working together to focus on near objects. Symptoms include eye strain, headaches, and difficulty reading. Treatment may involve vision therapy, prism glasses, or surgery.

What we help you with

Stroke/Paralysis

Stroke occurs when blood supply to the brain is interrupted, causing brain cells to die. Paralysis, the loss of muscle function or sensation, can result. Types include: Quadriplegia, Hemiplegia, Monoplegia

Balance and Mobility Issues

These problems affect a person's ability to move and maintain balance. They can be caused by various neurological conditions, injuries, or aging. Treatment often involves physical therapy and assistive devices.

Brain and Spinal Cord Injury

Injuries to the brain or spinal cord can lead to a range of impairments, including paralysis, sensory loss, cognitive difficulties, and emotional changes. Treatment focuses on rehabilitation and managing symptoms.

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

MS is an autoimmune disease that affects the central nervous system. It causes damage to the myelin sheath, leading to problems with communication between the brain and the body. Symptoms can vary widely and include fatigue, weakness, numbness, and vision problems.

Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's is a progressive neurological disorder that affects movement. It's caused by the death of dopamine-producing cells in the brain. Symptoms include tremors, stiffness, slowness of movement, and difficulty with balance and coordination

Cerebral Palsy

Cerebral palsy is a group of disorders that affect movement and posture. It's caused by damage to the developing brain, often before or during birth. Symptoms can vary widely and may include muscle weakness, spasticity, and difficulty with coordination.

Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)

GBS is a rare disorder in which the body's immune system attacks the nerves. This can lead to muscle weakness, paralysis, and sensory loss. In most cases, GBS is temporary, and people recover fully.

Ataxia

Ataxia is a neurological disorder that affects balance and coordination. It can be caused by various conditions, including stroke, brain injury, and certain genetic disorders. Symptoms include clumsiness, difficulty walking, and problems with fine motor skills.

Brachial Plexus Injury

The brachial plexus is a network of nerves that supplies sensation and movement to the arm and hand. Injury to this network can cause weakness, numbness, and pain in the arm and hand

Foot Drop and Wrist Drop

Foot drop and wrist drop are conditions in which the foot or wrist cannot be lifted properly. These conditions can be caused by nerve damage or muscle weakness.

Bell's Palsy

Bell's palsy is a condition that causes temporary weakness or paralysis of the muscles in the face. It's caused by damage to the facial nerve, which controls the muscles of the face.

Anxiety

A feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease, often accompanied by physical symptoms like rapid heartbeat, sweating, or difficulty breathing. When anxiety becomes excessive and interferes with daily life, it may be classified as an anxiety disorder.

Depression

A mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and worthlessness. Other symptoms include loss of interest in activities, changes in appetite and sleep patterns, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating.

Mood Swings/Irritability

These refer to rapid and extreme changes in mood, often involving periods of excessive happiness or anger. While occasional mood swings are normal, severe or frequent mood swings may indicate a mood disorder like bipolar disorder.

Insomnia

Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up too early. It can be caused by various factors, including stress, medical conditions, and certain medications.

Personality Disorders

Mental health conditions that involve long-term patterns of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that deviate significantly from cultural norms and expectations. They can impair a person's relationships, work, and ability to function in society.

Attention Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

A neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. 1 It can interfere with a person's ability to focus, organize tasks, and control their behavior.

Autism Spectrum Disorder

A developmental disorder that affects communication, social interaction, and behavior. People with ASD often have difficulty understanding social cues, engaging in back-and-forth conversations, and exhibiting repetitive behaviors.

Speech Disorders:

These are conditions that affect a person's ability to produce sounds or use language effectively. They can manifest in various ways, such as difficulty pronouncing words, stuttering, or problems understanding or using language.

Learning Disability

Down Syndrome / Autism

Down syndrome is a genetic condition caused by an extra chromosome, leading to developmental delays and physical characteristics. Autism spectrum disorder is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by challenges in social interaction, communication, and repetitive behaviors.

Speech Difficulty in Stroke
